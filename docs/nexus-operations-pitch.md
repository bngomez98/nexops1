## 3. THE PROBLEM

**The contractor matching industry operates on a model that benefits platforms financially while creating friction for both property owners and contractors.**

**The Property Owner Experience:**

A property manager submits a roofing repair request to a traditional platform. Within minutes, four to six contractors call—each having paid for the same lead. None have complete information about scope, budget, or timeline. The property manager repeats the same information to each, fields competing pitches, and ultimately decides based on whoever called first or quoted lowest—not on verified qualifications or fit. No documentation is produced. No audit trail exists.

**The Contractor Experience:**

A licensed roofing contractor pays $40-$80 for a lead. When they call, they discover three other contractors are calling simultaneously. The property owner is overwhelmed and unresponsive. The contractor invests 30-45 minutes attempting contact, assessment, and scheduling—only to learn the project went to someone who quoted less. They paid for a lead they had a 25% chance of winning and received nothing for time invested.

**Market Impact:**

A significant segment of Topeka's property management community has abandoned platforms entirely, returning to direct contractor relationships—not from preference, but because the platform experience was worse than the problem it claimed to solve.

**The Structural Issue:**

This is not a design problem solvable with better interfaces or faster response times. It is a structural incentive problem: platforms profit by selling the same lead to as many contractors as possible. They cannot introduce exclusivity without eliminating 70-80% of their lead revenue. The model cannot be fixed from within—it must be replaced.

## 4. OUR SOLUTION

**Nexus Operations is a two-sided coordination platform that assigns each project to exactly one verified contractor on an exclusive basis.**

When a contractor claims a project, it is permanently removed from every other contractor's view. No override capability exists. No re-listing is possible.

**What Nexus Operations Provides:**

- A coordinated platform where property owners submit complete project documentation once
- A verification infrastructure ensuring every contractor is licensed, insured, and background-checked before platform access
- A documentation engine producing timestamped audit trails for every project
- A confirmation process guaranteeing 24-hour response for consultation scheduling

**What Nexus Operations Is Not:**

- Not a lead generation service—project details are never sold to third parties
- Not a directory or referral platform—contractors do not compete for visibility or ranking
- Not a bidding environment—budget parameters are established before contractor assignment
- Not a per-lead pricing model—contractors pay flat monthly membership fees

**The Exclusive Assignment Mechanism:**

Exclusivity is enforced technically, not as policy. When a contractor claims a project, the platform's backend permanently removes that project from every other contractor's query results. There is no "unclaim" function. There is no administrative override. This is architecture, not a promise.

**Documentation Value:**

Every Nexus Operations project generates a complete, timestamped record including photographs, scope description, budget ceiling, contractor assignment, consultation confirmation, estimate submission, and decision record. For property managers who answer to property owners, boards, or compliance reviewers, this documentation provides professional defensibility that no competitor platform produces.

## 5. HOW IT WORKS

The process from project submission to confirmed consultation is designed to complete within 24 hours.

**Step 1 — Project Submission**

Property owners upload 2-10 photographs of the work area and provide a written scope description. They set a maximum budget ceiling before the project becomes visible to contractors. They select 3-4 available consultation windows.

**Example:**
- Service: Roofing
- Scope: "Leak in northwest corner following hailstorm. Water stain on ceiling in master bedroom. Roof is 18 years old, asphalt shingle."
- Budget Ceiling: $1,200
- Documentation: Exterior roof corner, interior ceiling stain, attic moisture evidence
- Availability: Thursday 2-4 PM, Friday 9-11 AM, Saturday 10 AM-12 PM

**Step 2 — Exclusive Contractor Assignment**

Verified contractors in the relevant trade and service area receive notification of the available project. They review complete submission details—photographs, scope, budget ceiling, and consultation windows—and decide whether the project fits their business.

One contractor claims it. The moment that claim executes, the project is permanently removed from every other contractor's view.

**Step 3 — Consultation Confirmation**

The assigned contractor commits to one consultation window. Both parties receive confirmation via email and SMS including contractor credentials, consultation details, and property address. A reminder is sent 24 hours prior.

The contractor arrives having reviewed photographs, read the scope, and noted the budget ceiling. The conversation begins at a professional level.

**Step 4 — Estimate Review and Decision**

The contractor provides a written estimate including scope of work, materials specifications, labor breakdown, timeline, and total cost. The property owner reviews and decides with no obligation to proceed. No penalty, fees, or pressure for declining.

The complete transaction—submission through decision—is documented with timestamps at every stage.

## 14. RISK ANALYSIS

**Risk 1: Pre-Revenue Status**

*Reality:* Zero paying customers to date.

*Root Cause:* Sales sequencing issue—property manager pipeline assembled before contractor supply confirmed.

*Mitigation:* Execute contractor-first outreach for 30-60 days before re-engaging property managers. Does not require capital; capital accelerates timeline.

*Validation:* First paying contractor and completed project within 60 days of funding.

**Risk 2: Two-Sided Cold Start**

*Reality:* Contractors reluctant to join without project volume; property managers reluctant to join without contractor supply.

*Mitigation:* Staged sequencing resolves this. Contractors join first because economics are independent of property manager volume—single claimed project justifies membership. Property managers join second with contractor supply demonstrated.

*Validation:* 5 paying contractors secured before first property manager outreach campaign.

**Risk 3: Contractor Churn**

*Reality:* Low project volume in early months could drive cancellations.

*Mitigation:* Focus on roofing and tree removal during peak Kansas spring/summer. Early contractors recruited with explicit expectation of modest but growing volume. Membership economics remain favorable even at low claim rates.

*Validation:* Target <20% churn in first 6 months.

**Risk 4: Geographic Concentration**

*Reality:* Single-market exposure creates risk if Topeka adoption is slower than projected.

*Mitigation:* Raise funds second-market expansion to Wichita by Month 15-18. Playbook is directly replicable.

*Validation:* Wichita contractor pipeline assembled by Month 15.

**Risk 5: Founder Dependency**

*Reality:* Limited team depth creates single-point-of-failure risk.

*Mitigation:* BD hire at Month 6 distributes responsibilities. Director of Operations provides continuity. Platform operates with minimal manual intervention once parties are onboarded.

*Validation:* Standard operating procedures documented by Month 6.
